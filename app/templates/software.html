Software Engineering

When I was younger I wanted to be an architect, so Legos and K'Nex were my creative tools. When I think about the structures I built then I'm reminded of my love for software. There's something special about making a dream a reality, and the things you learn along the way. Below are a few things I've worked on over the years and a few things I plan to do in the future.


My GitHub

I'm currently open sourcing the software I created during my dissertation work in the following steps:

  1. medmod-apis: A RESTful nodejs and docker based cloud service that manages and processes medical images and 3D models
  2. medmod-vr: A virtual reality viewer for medical images and 3D models (video)
  3. medmod-web: An electron and react based web application for uploading, managing and viewing medical images and 3D models
  4. medmod-sim: A virtual and mixed reality based catheter training simulator (video 1, video 2)
  5. medmod-mr: A mixed reality viewer for medical images and 3D models

A couple projects I contribute to:

  • True Nth: I developed authentication and security features for this web based portal that gives men with prostate cancer access to important tools
  • eSaude POC: I developed an automated end-to-end test framework for this point of care application for the Mozambique healthcare system. This application is built on the OpenMRS electronic health record system.

University of Washington PhD Candidate in Biomedical and Health Informatics

I'm researching ways to use medical image processing, virtual reality (VR), mixed reality (MR), 3D modeling and 3D printing to decentralize catheterization procedures.

Research Interests:
  • VR & MR Procedural Guidance: I'm investigating how VR and MR can improve catheterization procedures by comparing conventional procedures to those performed in a novel simulator (see medmod-sim under My GitHub above) using medical image processing, VR, MR, sensors and 3D printing (video 1, video 2).
  • VR and MR Surgical Planning and Education: How do VR and MR influence surgical planning and medical education? I'm exploring this question in novel VR and MR 3D model viewers (see medmod-vr and medmod-mr un My GitHub above) with physicians at Seattle Children's, UC Irvine and University of Washington (video)
  • Self Driving Catheters: I can't wait to explore how realtime ultrasound image processing, AI and robotics can programmatically guide catheters. I hope this research helps improve who has access to safe catheter based treatments, because far too few do.
Selected Publications:
  • Angiography-Free Delivery of Transcatheter Arterial Embolization Using Virtual Reality Guidance
  • Initial Experience with Renal Virtual Reality Models as Educational and Preoperative Planning Tools for Partial Nephrectomy
  • Virtual-Reality Projected Renal Models with Urolithiasis as an Educational and Preoperative Planning Tool for Nephrolithotomy: A Pilot Study
  • Semantics-Based Composition of Integrated Cardiomyocyte Models Motivated by Real-World Use Cases

Microsoft Office Full Stack Engineer

I worked as a full stack engineer on the following Microsoft Office products:

OneNote
  • Cloud Services: Collaborating with OneDrive, I developed, tested and maintained RESTful APIs in C# on Azure for OneNote client applications. This introduced me to the power and importance of cloud services.
  • Authentication: I developed an OAuth authentication service for OneNote client users. This showed me the importance of security and power of OAuth and similar technologies.
  • Website: I developed the first version of dev.onenote.com using Typescript and ASP.NET
  • Testing: I developed unit and end-to-end tests for my features. This taught me how tests speed up development despite having a large upfront cost.
Access
  • Team: Access 2010 was the best dev team I've ever worked on. This experience showed me the importance of the people to the product.
  • SQL: I developed several features that stored and retrieved information in SQL. This showed me the benefits and drawbacks of a structured DB.
  • C++, C#, Javascript: I developed several features using a few languages that cross-communicated. This taught me that languages are like tools on a tool belt that should be used together to build efficiently.

University of Michigan Computer Science Engineering

I received a B.S. in computer science from the University of Michigan in 2010 where I had many great experiences:

  • CUDA Medical Image Processing: I helped develop a CUDA module that processed brain images on a GPU. This experience showed me the power of GPU processing and its potential to improve healthcare.
  • Object Oriented Programming: My toughest class at UMich taught me OOP in C++. We created std classes from scratch and used them to create a command line game. After this class I was sure I picked the right field.
  • Computer Architecture: In this class I learned how software interacts with hardware and how to think about writing performant applications.
  • Football: This has nothing to do with software, but it's worth mentioning that I will forever bleed maize and blue. Umich has the best student section in the nation in my completely unbiased opinion. Go blue!
