I'm researching ways to use medical image processing, virtual reality (VR), mixed reality (MR), 3D modeling and 3D printing to decentralize catheterization procedures.

Research Interests:
  • VR & MR Procedural Guidance: I'm investigating how VR and MR can improve catheterization procedures by comparing conventional procedures to those performed in a novel simulator (see medmod-sim under My GitHub above) using medical image processing, VR, MR, sensors and 3D printing (video 1, video 2).
  • VR and MR Surgical Planning and Education: How do VR and MR influence surgical planning and medical education? I'm exploring this question in novel VR and MR 3D model viewers (see medmod-vr and medmod-mr un My GitHub above) with physicians at Seattle Children's, UC Irvine and University of Washington (video)
  • Self Driving Catheters: I can't wait to explore how realtime ultrasound image processing, AI and robotics can programmatically guide catheters. I hope this research helps improve who has access to safe catheter based treatments, because far too few do.
Selected Publications:

  • Initial Experience with Renal Virtual Reality Models as Educational and Preoperative Planning Tools for Partial Nephrectomy
  • Virtual-Reality Projected Renal Models with Urolithiasis as an Educational and Preoperative Planning Tool for Nephrolithotomy: A Pilot Study
  • Semantics-Based Composition of Integrated Cardiomyocyte Models Motivated by Real-World Use Cases
